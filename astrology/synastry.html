Synastry

Synastry is the branch of astrology that compares 2 birth charts to reveal compatibility, attraction, tension, and the deeper dynamics between 2 people.

| What Is Synastry?

Synastry is the art of comparing 2 natal charts to understand how 2 people relate to each other. Rather than looking at a single chart in isolation, synastry compares one person's chart with another's to show how their planetary placements interact.

One core technique is examining how one person's planets fall into the other person's houses. For example, if your Venus falls in someone else's 7th house, it may activate themes of partnership, attraction, and relating in that area of their life. This kind of house overlay helps reveal where connection, support, and tension may arise between 2 people.

The aspects between the 2 charts are equally important. Conjunctions, trines, squares, oppositions, and sextiles between one person's planets and the other's show how their energies blend, challenge, or amplify each other. Together, house overlays and inter-chart aspects form the foundation of a synastry reading.

| How Synastry Works

Planet-to-Planet Aspects

The aspects between 2 charts are a primary tool of synastry. Conjunctions merge energies, trines create easy flow, sextiles offer supportive opportunities, squares generate friction and growth, and oppositions create attraction alongside tension. Each aspect type colors the relationship differently.

House Overlays

House overlays show where one person's planets fall in the other person's life areas. If someone's Mars falls in your 10th house, their drive and ambition may directly affect your career, direction, or public life. These overlays reveal which themes each person activates in the other.

Orb and Weight

Not all aspects carry equal weight. Tighter orbs, aspects closer to exact, are usually stronger. Aspects involving personal planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, and Mars) are often felt more directly than aspects involving outer planets alone.

Reciprocal Analysis

Synastry works both ways. Your Venus on someone's Ascendant is a different experience from their Venus on yours. A thorough synastry reading looks at the interaction in both directions to understand how each person experiences the other.

| Key Synastry Aspects

Sun-Moon Aspects

Sun-Moon contacts are among the most important in synastry. They often reflect emotional compatibility and a sense of mutual recognition. When one person's Sun aspects the other's Moon, there is often a natural feeling of connection, understanding, and familiarity between them.

Venus-Mars Aspects

Venus-Mars aspects are strongly associated with attraction and romantic chemistry. These contacts show how desire and affection move between 2 people. Conjunctions and trines often create strong magnetic pull, while squares can produce a passionate but more volatile dynamic.

Moon-Moon Aspects

Moon-Moon contacts reveal emotional resonance. When 2 people's Moons are in harmonious aspect, they often feel emotionally safe and understood by each other. Challenging Moon-Moon aspects can point to different emotional needs, habits, and rhythms.

Saturn Aspects

Saturn contacts in synastry speak to commitment, durability, and structure. Harmonious Saturn aspects can help create a stable, lasting bond. Challenging Saturn aspects may bring feelings of restriction, pressure, obligation, or limitation. Saturn often shows where a relationship requires patience and maturity.

Pluto Aspects

Pluto contacts bring intensity and transformation. These aspects can indicate deep attraction and a relationship that changes both people profoundly. Pluto synastry aspects often surface power dynamics and trigger personal evolution, for better or for worse.

| Synastry vs. Composite Charts

Synastry and composite charts are both tools for understanding relationships, but they approach the question from different angles.

Synastry

Compares two individual charts side by side. Shows how each person experiences the other. Focuses on inter-chart aspects and house overlays. Best for understanding the dynamic between two separate people and how their energies interact.

Composite Charts

Blends both charts into a single chart that represents the relationship as its own entity. Shows the purpose, themes, and challenges of the relationship itself. Best for understanding what the relationship is about as a whole, rather than how each person experiences it individually.

Both methods are valuable. Synastry shows how 2 people affect each other. Composite charts show what the relationship itself represents. Many astrologers use both together for a more complete picture.

| Frequently Asked Questions

What is synastry in astrology?
Synastry is the branch of relationship astrology that compares two natal birth charts to understand the dynamics between two people. One person's planets are placed in the other person's houses, and the aspects between the two charts reveal how their energies interact, where attraction exists, and where friction may arise.
What is the difference between synastry and a composite chart?
Synastry compares two individual charts side by side, examining how one person's planets interact with the other's. A composite chart blends both charts into a single chart that represents the relationship itself as its own entity. Both approaches are useful and serve different purposes in relationship astrology.
What are the most important synastry aspects to look for?
Key synastry aspects include Sun-Moon contacts for emotional compatibility, Venus-Mars aspects for physical attraction and chemistry, Moon-Moon aspects for emotional resonance and comfort, Saturn aspects for commitment and long-term stability, and Pluto aspects for transformation and intensity in the relationship.
Can synastry predict whether a relationship will work?
Synastry reveals the mechanics of attraction and compatibility, not the outcome. It shows where two people naturally connect and where challenges may arise. How a relationship unfolds depends on the awareness, maturity, and choices of the individuals involved. Synastry is a tool for understanding dynamics, not a guarantee of success or failure.
