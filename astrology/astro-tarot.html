The bridge between astrology and tarot. Discover which tarot cards correspond to your zodiac signs, planets, and birth chart placements.

| What Is Astro Tarot?

Astro Tarot is the bridge between two of the world's oldest symbolic systems. Astrology and tarot have been intertwined since at least the Renaissance, when scholars and occultists began mapping correspondences between the zodiac, the planets, and the tarot deck. Every major card in the tarot has an astrological assignment, and every sign, planet, and element in astrology has a tarot counterpart.

Understanding these correspondences deepens both practices. If you know your birth chart, tarot gives you a visual, narrative framework for understanding your placements. If you read tarot, astrology gives you a systematic structure behind each card's meaning.

Genetic Matrix takes this connection further by generating a personalized Astro Tarot chart that maps your specific birth chart placements to their corresponding tarot cards. This gives you a unique spread of cards that reflects your astrological blueprint, a tarot portrait of your natal chart.

| The Major Arcana and the Zodiac

The 22 Major Arcana cards correspond to the 12 zodiac signs, the 7 classical planets, and the 3 elements (plus The Fool, which stands outside the system as pure potential).

Zodiac Sign Correspondences

The Emperor: Aries. Authority, structure, leadership, and the will to build. The Emperor channels Aries' cardinal fire into the creation of order and governance.

The Hierophant: Taurus. Tradition, teaching, spiritual authority, and established wisdom. The Hierophant reflects Taurus' connection to enduring values and material-spiritual integration.

The Lovers: Gemini. Choice, duality, communication, and relationship. The Lovers card captures Gemini's essential theme of two paths, two perspectives, and the decision between them.

The Chariot: Cancer. Emotional determination, protective drive, and the will to carry forward. The Chariot reflects Cancer's cardinal water energy, moving forward through feeling rather than force.

Strength: Leo. Courage, heart, inner power, and the gentle mastery of raw force. Strength shows Leo at its highest expression, leading through warmth and presence rather than dominance.

The Hermit: Virgo. Solitude, analysis, inner wisdom, and the search for truth through careful examination. The Hermit reflects Virgo's journey inward, seeking perfection through understanding rather than action.

Justice: Libra. Balance, fairness, truth, and the consequences of choices. Justice mirrors Libra's core drive for equilibrium and ethical clarity.

Death: Scorpio. Transformation, endings, release, and rebirth. The Death card captures Scorpio's fundamental truth: something must die for something new to live.

Temperance: Sagittarius. Integration, patience, higher purpose, and the alchemy of combining opposites. Temperance reflects Sagittarius' search for meaning through the synthesis of experience.

The Devil: Capricorn. Materialism, bondage, shadow, and the power structures that both serve and trap us. The Devil shows Capricorn's relationship with ambition, authority, and the shadow side of worldly success.

The Star: Aquarius. Hope, inspiration, humanitarian vision, and the connection to universal truth. The Star reflects Aquarius' idealism and its role as the sign that pours wisdom out for the collective.

The Moon: Pisces. Illusion, intuition, the subconscious, and the journey through darkness toward understanding. The Moon card captures Pisces' oceanic depth, the realm of dreams, fears, and spiritual longing.

Planetary Correspondences

The Fool: Uranus (and the element of Air). Pure potential, the leap of faith, and the beginning of all journeys.

The Magician: Mercury. Skill, communication, willpower, and the ability to channel spiritual energy into material results.

The High Priestess: The Moon. Intuition, mystery, the subconscious mind, and hidden knowledge waiting to be accessed.

The Empress: Venus. Abundance, fertility, beauty, nurturing, and the creative power of nature.

The Wheel of Fortune: Jupiter. Expansion, cycles, luck, and the turning of fate that brings growth through change.

The Tower: Mars. Sudden disruption, revelation, the destruction of false structures, and the raw energy of breakthrough.

The Sun: The Sun. Joy, clarity, vitality, success, and the radiant truth of authentic self-expression.

Judgement: Pluto. Awakening, reckoning, transformation, and the call to rise into a higher version of yourself.

The World: Saturn. Completion, mastery, integration, and the fulfillment that comes from disciplined effort and earned wisdom.

Key Insight

Every major card in the tarot has an astrological assignment. The 22 Major Arcana correspond to the 12 zodiac signs, the classical planets, and the elements. Understanding these correspondences deepens both your astrology and tarot practice.

| The Minor Arcana and the Elements

The four suits of the Minor Arcana correspond to the four astrological elements:

Wands: Fire (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius). Action, passion, creativity, ambition, and spiritual drive. Wand cards describe the experiences associated with fire energy: inspiration, competition, adventure, and the will to create.

Cups: Water (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces). Emotion, intuition, relationships, and the inner world. Cup cards describe experiences of love, grief, joy, dreams, and the depths of feeling.

Swords: Air (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius). Thought, communication, conflict, and truth. Sword cards describe mental experiences: clarity, confusion, decision-making, and the pain and power of intellectual honesty.

Pentacles: Earth (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn). Material reality, work, money, health, and practical achievement. Pentacle cards describe experiences of building, earning, growing, and engaging with the physical world.

Each numbered card (Ace through 10) corresponds to a specific 10-degree segment of the zodiac (called a decan), linking individual cards to precise astrological positions.
