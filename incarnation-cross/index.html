Foundation · Incarnation Cross

The 192 Incarnation Crosses

In Human Design, your Incarnation Cross describes the major theme of your incarnation. It is defined by the four Gate activations of your Personality Sun, Personality Earth, Design Sun, and Design Earth, and these Crosses are organized through the four Quarters of the Mandala and the three Cross geometries.

| What Is an Incarnation Cross?

Your Incarnation Cross is determined by four specific Gate activations in your chart: your Personality Sun Gate, Personality Earth Gate, Design Sun Gate, and Design Earth Gate. Together, these four Gates form the core thematic structure of your Incarnation Cross.

The Personality Sun Gate keys the Incarnation Cross to its Quarter and gives the Cross its dominant conscious orientation, while the Sun and Earth Lines define its geometry. Even so, the Cross is not determined by the Personality Sun alone. An Incarnation Cross is constituted by all four Gates, and the other three are not simply adjectives modifying the Sun Gate, but essential elements of the Cross's complete thematic structure.

There are 192 Incarnation Crosses in total. Each of the 64 Gates can serve as the Personality Sun Gate, and each produces three geometry variations: a Right Angle Cross, a Left Angle Cross, and a Juxtaposition Cross, giving 64 × 3 = 192.

| The Three Cross Geometries

Right Angle Cross (RAX)

Personal

Right Angle Crosses carry a personal geometry. Their trajectory is primarily personal rather than transpersonal. The life theme is self-contained and is not oriented through interpersonal exchange in the same way as a Left Angle Cross.

Left Angle Cross (LAX)

Transpersonal

Left Angle Crosses carry a transpersonal geometry. Their purpose tends to unfold through interaction, exchange, and relationships, reflecting a life theme that is worked out through contact with others rather than in isolation.

Juxtaposition Cross (JX)

Bridge

Juxtaposition Crosses serve as a strong bridge between Right Angle and Left Angle geometries. Their life theme tends to express itself in a specific and stable way, linking the personal and transpersonal.

| The Four Quarters

The 64 Gates are organized around the Incarnation Mandala into four Quarters, each containing 16 Gates. The Quarter your Personality Sun falls in determines the broad thematic landscape of your Cross. Each Quarter represents a distinct thematic orientation within the Incarnation Mandala, shaping the broader context through which a Cross expresses itself.

Quarter 1: Initiation

The Quarter of Mind

The purpose of mental awareness. Gates 13, 49, 30, 55, 37, 63, 22, 36, 25, 17, 21, 51, 42, 03, 27, 24. Crosses in this Quarter are oriented toward mental awareness, inquiry, and the initiation of understanding.

Quarter 2: Civilization

The Quarter of Form

The purpose of form and material life. Gates 02, 23, 08, 20, 16, 35, 45, 12, 15, 52, 39, 53, 62, 56, 31, 33. Crosses in this Quarter are oriented toward form, structure, material life, and the expression of ideas in the world.

Quarter 3: Duality

The Quarter of Awareness

The purpose of bonding and relationship. Gates 07, 04, 29, 59, 40, 64, 47, 06, 46, 18, 48, 57, 32, 50, 28, 44. Crosses in this Quarter are oriented toward relationship, bonding, and awareness through interaction with others.

Quarter 4: Mutation

The Quarter of Energy

The purpose of transformation. Gates 01, 43, 14, 34, 09, 05, 26, 11, 10, 58, 38, 54, 61, 60, 41, 19. Crosses in this Quarter are oriented toward transformation, mutation, and the energy for evolutionary change.

| Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Incarnation Cross in Human Design?
In Human Design, the Incarnation Cross describes the major theme of your incarnation. It is defined by the four Gate activations of your Personality Sun, Personality Earth, Design Sun, and Design Earth. Together, these four placements form the Cross and provide a framework for understanding the broader purpose and orientation of the chart.
What is the difference between Right Angle, Left Angle, and Juxtaposition Crosses?
Right Angle Crosses carry a personal geometry. Their trajectory is primarily personal rather than transpersonal. Left Angle Crosses carry a transpersonal geometry. Their purpose tends to unfold through interaction, exchange, and relationships, reflecting a life theme that is worked out through contact with others rather than in isolation. Juxtaposition Crosses serve as a strong bridge between Right Angle and Left Angle geometries. Their life theme tends to express itself in a specific and stable way, linking the personal and transpersonal.
What are the four Quarters in the Incarnation Cross?
The four Quarters organize the 64 Gates around the Mandala. Quarter 1, Initiation, is associated with Mind and themes of mental awareness and purpose. Quarter 2, Civilization, is associated with Form and themes of material and societal structure. Quarter 3, Duality, is associated with Awareness and themes of bonding and relationship. Quarter 4, Mutation, is associated with Energy and themes of transformation, mutation, and evolutionary change.
How do I find my Incarnation Cross?
Your Incarnation Cross is determined by your birth data. Generate a free Human Design chart at Genetic Matrix and your Incarnation Cross will be displayed. It is calculated from the positions of your Personality Sun, Personality Earth, Design Sun, and Design Earth Gates.
How many Incarnation Crosses are there?
There are 192 Incarnation Crosses in Human Design. Each of the 64 Gates can serve as the Personality Sun Gate of a Cross, and each Gate produces three geometry variations: a Right Angle Cross, a Left Angle Cross, and a Juxtaposition Cross, for a total of 192 Crosses.
Does my Incarnation Cross change over time?
No. Your Incarnation Cross is fixed at birth and does not change over time.
What is the Personality Sun Gate and why is it important?
The Personality Sun Gate keys the Incarnation Cross to its Quarter and gives the Cross its dominant conscious orientation. However, the Incarnation Cross is constituted by all four Gates, not the Personality Sun alone.
Can two people have the same Incarnation Cross but express it differently?
Yes. The Incarnation Cross is a theme, not a script. Two people with the same Cross may share the same underlying life theme, but how that theme is expressed depends on the full chart, including Type, Authority, Profile, defined Channels, and the specific Lines of the Gate activations. No two charts are identical, so no two expressions of the same Cross will be exactly alike.
