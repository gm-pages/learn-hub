The 9 Centers

The nine Centers are the architecture of your Bodygraph. Each one is a hub of energy with a specific biological and psychological function, and together they determine your Type, your definition, and the way you process the world around you.

| Tools, not doctrine.

The Human Design system first emerged at the beginning of 1987. Genetic Matrix provides these tools for your own exploration and experiment. The Centers are where you begin to see the mechanics of your design in action - where energy flows reliably and where you are open to conditioning.

| What Are Centers?

In the Human Design System, the Bodygraph contains nine Centers, each corresponding to specific biological functions and psychological themes. These Centers are derived from the Hindu-Brahmin Chakra system but are adapted to reflect the evolution of human consciousness since 1781 - when the seven-centered being gave way to the nine-centered being we are today.

Each Center is either defined (colored in) or undefined/open (white) in your Bodygraph. A defined Center has consistent, reliable energy - it operates the same way regardless of who is around you. An undefined or open Center takes in and amplifies the energy of others, making it a place of deep conditioning and potential wisdom.

The specific combination of defined and undefined Centers in your chart determines your Type. Whether your Sacral Center is defined, whether you have a motor connected to the Throat, and whether you have any definition at all - these are the mechanical foundations that create the five Types. Understanding your Centers is understanding the architecture of your energy.

Defined Centers are who you are - consistent and reliable. Undefined and open Centers are where you go to school in this life. They are not weaknesses; they are places of extraordinary sensitivity and, ultimately, wisdom. The key is recognizing the difference between what is yours and what you are taking in from the world.

| The 9 Centers

Head Center
Pineal gland · Top of the Bodygraph

The Head Center is one of two pressure centers in the Bodygraph, creating mental pressure in the form of inspiration and questions. When defined, you have a consistent and reliable way of being inspired - a fixed mental pressure that drives you to think about and question specific things. When undefined, you take in and amplify the mental pressure of everyone around you, which can feel like being overwhelmed by questions and ideas that are not yours. The not-self of the undefined Head tries to answer everyone else's questions and resolve pressure that was never theirs to resolve.

| Defined, Undefined and Open

Every center in your Bodygraph exists in one of three states. Understanding these states is fundamental to knowing where your energy is reliable and where you are subject to conditioning from the world around you.

Defined Centers

A defined center is colored in on your Bodygraph. It has at least one complete channel connecting it to another defined center, creating a consistent and reliable flow of energy. Defined centers represent who you are - this energy operates the same way regardless of who is around you. You radiate this energy outward and condition others with it. Roughly speaking, defined centers are where you are fixed, consistent, and trustworthy to yourself.

Undefined Centers

An undefined center appears white on your Bodygraph but has one or more gate activations within it. You do not have consistent access to this center's energy, but the activated gates give you a partial, hanging connection - a hook that draws in energy from others. Undefined centers are where you take in, amplify, and reflect the energy of defined centers around you. They are your greatest classrooms, offering wisdom through experience over time.

Open Centers

A completely open center is also white on your Bodygraph but has no gate activations at all. This is the deepest point of conditioning - and the deepest potential for wisdom. With no gates to filter or focus the incoming energy, an open center takes in everything and can experience the full spectrum of that center's theme. Open centers are where you can become truly wise about energy that is not your own, precisely because you have no fixed way of processing it.

In practice, people often use "undefined" and "open" interchangeably. The important distinction is between defined and not-defined. If a center is white on your chart, you are taking in and amplifying energy there - whether it is technically undefined (with gates) or completely open (no gates). The wisdom potential is the same; the depth of conditioning may differ.

| Common Questions

What is the difference between undefined and open centers?
An undefined center has one or more gate activations but no complete channel, giving you a partial hook into that center's energy. A completely open center has no gate activations at all. Both appear white on the Bodygraph and both take in and amplify energy from defined centers around them. The practical difference is subtle: open centers have no filter for incoming energy, while undefined centers have some gates that color how that energy is received. In everyday discussion, the terms are often used interchangeably.
Can my centers change over time?
No. Your Bodygraph definition is determined at the moment of birth and remains fixed throughout your life. However, planetary transits continuously activate gates and channels, which can temporarily define centers on any given day. This is why your energy can feel different depending on the transit weather. Your natal definition - the foundation of your design - never changes.
Is it bad to have undefined or open centers?
Not at all. Undefined and open centers are your greatest source of potential wisdom. They are where you go to school in this life - taking in, amplifying, and learning about energy that is not consistently yours. The challenge is not-self conditioning: making decisions as if this amplified energy were your own. When you learn to recognize what is yours and what you are taking in, your open centers become places of extraordinary sensitivity, discernment, and wisdom.
How many centers does each Type typically have defined?
It varies significantly. Generators and Manifesting Generators always have the Sacral center defined, plus at least one other center connected by a channel. Manifestors have a motor center connected to the Throat (but no defined Sacral). Projectors have no defined Sacral and no motor-to-Throat connection, though they can have many other centers defined. Reflectors have no defined centers at all - all nine centers are open. Beyond these Type-defining mechanics, the number of additional defined centers varies from person to person.
Do transits affect my centers?
Yes. The planets are constantly moving through the gates of the Bodygraph, and when a transiting gate completes a channel with one of your natal gates, it temporarily defines a center. This is known as transit conditioning. For Reflectors especially, who have no fixed definition, the daily transit field is the primary way they experience definition. For all Types, understanding the transit weather can help explain shifts in energy and mood.
How do I know if I am experiencing not-self conditioning?
Not-self conditioning shows up as consistent patterns of behavior driven by your undefined or open centers. Each open center has specific not-self questions you can ask yourself. For example: Am I trying to answer questions that are not mine? (open Head). Am I pretending to be certain? (open Ajna). Am I trying to attract attention? (open Throat). Am I trying to prove my worth? (open Heart). The key signal is making decisions from pressure, amplification, or energy that is not reliably yours. Following your Strategy and Authority is the primary antidote to not-self conditioning.
