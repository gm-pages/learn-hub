The Circuit Families

Circuits group channels into families that share a common theme. Individual circuits mutate. Collective circuits share. Tribal circuits support. Understanding which circuits are active in your chart reveals the broader purpose operating through your definition.

| Tools, not doctrine.

The Human Design System was transmitted through Ra Uru Hu beginning in 1987. Genetic Matrix provides these tools for your own exploration and experiment. Circuits reveal the broader themes that organize the bodygraph's 36 channels into functional families - each with a distinct purpose.

| What Are Circuits?

A circuit is a group of channels in the bodygraph that share a common theme and purpose. While each channel has its own specific energy, the circuit it belongs to provides the broader context for how that energy operates. Circuits are the organizational layer between individual channels and the whole bodygraph.

The 36 channels are organized into three main circuit groups - Individual, Collective, and Tribal - plus the Integration channels, which stand on their own. Each group has sub-circuits that carry more specific themes within the broader family.

Understanding your circuitry tells you something fundamental: are you here to mutate and empower (Individual), to share and contribute (Collective), or to support and provide (Tribal)? Most people carry channels from multiple circuit groups, creating a unique blend of these life themes.

| Individual Circuit Group

The Individual circuitry carries the theme of mutation and empowerment. Individual energy is acoustic - it transforms through frequency rather than explanation. People with strong Individual circuitry march to their own beat. They are not here to share or support; they are here to be unique and, in that uniqueness, to mutate the world around them. The Individual circuit group contains two sub-circuits: Knowing and Centering.

Knowing Circuit

The Knowing Circuit carries the theme of individual knowing - insights and awareness that arise without logical explanation. This is intuitive, mutative energy. The channels in this circuit connect the Head and Ajna to the Throat, and the Root and Solar Plexus to the Throat and Spleen, creating pathways for inner knowing to be expressed or recognized.

| Collective Circuit Group

The Collective circuitry carries the theme of sharing. Collective energy is designed to be shared with everyone - it is not personal. There are two very different ways the Collective shares: through logical patterns (Understanding) and through experiential cycles (Sensing). Together, these two circuits account for the largest number of channels in the bodygraph.

Understanding Circuit (Logic)

The Understanding Circuit, also called the Logic Circuit, carries the theme of patterns and proof. This circuitry processes information through recognizing repeatable patterns - testing, validating, and projecting into the future based on what can be proven. It is oriented toward what might work for everyone. Logic is future-focused and driven by the need for correction and improvement.

| Tribal Circuit Group

The Tribal circuitry carries the theme of support, loyalty, and resources. Tribal energy is fundamentally about making deals - it operates through bargains, agreements, and material bonds. Where Individual energy mutates and Collective energy shares, Tribal energy protects and provides. It is the most ancient circuitry, rooted in survival through community. The Tribal group contains two sub-circuits: Ego and Defense.

Tribal Circuit (Ego)

The Tribal Circuit (sometimes called the Ego Circuit) is centered around the Heart/Will Center and carries the theme of willpower, material resources, and community bonds. This circuitry drives the tribe's material well-being through deals, contracts, and commitments. The Heart Center's willpower is the engine of this circuit - it is the energy to make promises and keep them.

| Integration Channels

The Integration Channels stand apart from the three circuit groups. They are not part of Individual, Collective, or Tribal circuitry. These four channels are focused purely on individual survival and self-empowerment. They connect the Sacral, Spleen, G Center, and Throat in a tight cluster that is about being equipped to survive on your own. Integration energy is the most self-contained circuitry in the bodygraph.

People with strong Integration definition often need to develop self-reliance before they can effectively engage with others.

| Common Questions

What is a circuit in Human Design?
A circuit is a group of channels in the bodygraph that share a common theme. Circuits organize the 36 channels into families based on how they function. The three main circuit groups are Individual (mutation and empowerment), Collective (sharing through logic and experience), and Tribal (support and resources). The four Integration channels stand alone, focused on personal survival. Understanding your circuitry reveals the broader purpose operating through your defined channels.
How many circuits are there in Human Design?
There are seven circuits organized into three main groups, plus the Integration channels. The Individual group contains the Knowing Circuit (7 channels) and the Centering Circuit (4 channels). The Collective group contains the Understanding/Logic Circuit (6 channels) and the Sensing/Abstract Circuit (8 channels). The Tribal group contains the Ego Circuit (5 channels) and the Defense Circuit (2 channels). The four Integration channels stand on their own outside any circuit group.
What is the difference between Individual, Collective, and Tribal circuits?
Individual circuits carry the theme of mutation and empowerment - unique knowing and creative expression that transforms without explanation. Collective circuits carry the theme of sharing - either through logical patterns (Understanding) or experiential cycles (Sensing). Tribal circuits carry the theme of support - resource management, loyalty, and community bonds. Each group processes and moves energy in fundamentally different ways, and most people carry channels from multiple groups.
What are the Integration channels?
The Integration channels are four channels focused purely on individual survival and self-empowerment: the Channel of the Brainwave (20-57), the Channel of Charisma (20-34), the Channel of Power (34-57), and the Channel of Exploration (10-34). They do not belong to any circuit group and represent the most self-focused energy in the bodygraph. People with Integration channels often need to develop strong self-reliance before they can effectively connect with others.
Does the circuit a channel belongs to change its meaning?
Yes. The circuit provides the broader context for how a channel's energy operates. A channel in the Individual circuit works through mutation and acoustic frequency - transforming without needing to explain. That same energy in a Collective circuit would be about sharing patterns or experiences with everyone. Through a Tribal circuit, it would be about loyalty and material support for the community. The circuit is the theme within which each channel fulfills its specific function.
