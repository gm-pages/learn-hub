Tropical

Tropical Zodiac

The standard system specified by Ra Uru Hu for Human Design. Based on the seasons and the vernal equinox, this is the most widely used system in Western astrology.

Tropical

J2000 Reference Frame

A standard celestial reference frame that freezes the mean equator and equinox at 12:00 Terrestrial Time on January 1, 2000. Removes Precession and Nutation.

Sidereal

Sidereal Calculation

Unlike the tropical zodiac, sidereal systems measure time or motion based on the position of the Earth relative to the distant, "fixed" stars, rather than relative to the Sun. To account for the Earth's precession of the equinoxes, Ayanamshas are used ensuring that the zodiac signs align with the actual, fixed constellations in the sky.

Draconic Zodiac

The Draconic system shifts the entire tropical zodiac so the North Node becomes 0° Aries. It reveals a soul-level chart connected to your karmic path.

Core Astrology

Zodiac Signs

The 12 Zodiac signs - plus the controversial True Sidereal 13th sign Ophiuchus. Explore each sign's element, modality, ruling planet, and core characteristics.

From the Sun through personal planets like Mercury and Mars to outer planets like Pluto, understand what each celestial body represents in your birth chart.

Houses & House Systems

The 12 houses represent the specific areas or "stages" of life where different energies (planets) play out, acting as a map for your daily experiences and developmental themes. There are over a dozen recognised house systems all supported by Genetic Matrix - from Placidus and Koch to Whole Sign and Equal.

Explore →

Aspects & Orbs

Aspects in astrology represent the geometric angles and relationships between planets. They reveal how planetary energies interact, influencing personality dynamics, potentials, and challenges through specific angular distances, such as 0°, 90°, or 120°.

Explore →

Asteroids & Planetoids

Asteroids and Planetoids (eg Ceres) act as "messengers" or archetypal influencers, often linked to mythology, that provide deeper insights into relationships, personality, and life events. Common Asteroids and Planetoids include Ceres (nurturing), Pallas (wisdom), Juno (partnership), and Vesta (devotion).

Explore →

Fixed Stars

Fixed stars are used in astrology to add specific, high-intensity, and often karmic, energetic nuances to natal charts, usually acting through close conjunctions with planets or angles like the Ascendant or Midheaven. Unlike planets, which signify personal or temporary, changing influences, these stationary stars provide enduring, often dramatic life themes, talents, or challenges.

Explore →

Ayanamsha Systems

Ayanamsha is the angular difference between the tropical zodiac (based on the seasons) and the sidereal zodiac (based on the fixed stars). It is also the "correction factor" used in Vedic astrology (Jyotish) to align the zodiac with the actual current positions of the stars, accounting for the precession of the equinoxes. Explore 50+ ayanamsha systems supported by Genetic Matrix, from Lahiri to custom.

Explore →

Synastry

Synastry is the branch of relationship astrology that compares two people's birth charts to determine their compatibility, strengths, and potential friction areas. It acts as a "cosmic map" that overlays one person's planets onto another's to show how their energies interact.

Explore →

Progressions

Astrological progressions are a technique used to forecast internal psychological development, personal evolution, and major life themes over time. Progressions represent how your birth chart evolves and matures from the inside out. Genetic Matrix supports Secondary Progressions and Solar Arc Directions.

Explore →

Additional Astrology

Sabian Symbols

The Sabian Symbols are a set of 360 distinct, metaphorical images used in astrology to add deep, intuitive, and symbolic meaning to a birth chart or to act as an oracle for guidance. They represent a "living language" of images rather than rigid, technical rules.

Explore →

Charubel Degrees

Charubel Degrees are a set of 360 unique, often fatalistic, symbolic interpretations for each individual degree of the zodiac, developed by the 19th-century Welsh clairvoyant and astrologer John Thomas (under the pseudonym Charubel).

Explore →

La Volasfera (Sepharial)

La Volasfera is a set of 360 degree interpretations published by the astrologer Sepharial, offering vivid symbolic descriptions for each degree of the zodiac based on esoteric and divinatory tradition.

Explore →

Astro Tarot

Astrology and Tarot are linked as sister systems of divination that use shared archetypal symbols, elements, and cosmic energy to reveal, analyze, and map personal life paths. Astrology offers the overarching cosmic "map" of planetary movements, while Tarot provides the specific, detailed "design" for individual situations.

Explore →

Chinese Astrology

Chinese astrology is an ancient system based on a 12-year lunar cycle, where each year corresponds to an animal sign (Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, Pig). It predicts personality and fate based on birth year, the five elements (wood, fire, earth, metal, water), and Yin/Yang, forming a 60-year cycle. Genetic Matrix integrates Chinese astrology with Western systems for a multi-system view.

Explore →

Vedic Astrology

Vedic Astrology, known in Sanskrit as Jyotish, is an ancient Indian system that maps the positions of stars and planets at the exact moment of a person's birth to understand their life path, personality, and karma. Unlike Western astrology, it uses the sidereal zodiac (aligned with actual constellations) and relies heavily on the Moon sign and 27 lunar mansions (Nakshatras). Genetic Matrix supports North Indian, South Indian, and Eastern chart styles and methods.
